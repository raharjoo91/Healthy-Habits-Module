🛡️ Relapse Prevention Strategies

Identify High-Risk Situations & Plan Ahead

Click on each scenario to develop personalized strategies for staying on track during challenging times

🎉

Social Gatherings

Parties, holidays, and celebrations with tempting foods and peer pressure

Your Action Plan:

🥗 Eat a healthy snack before going
💧 Bring a water bottle and stay hydrated
🤝 Find a supportive friend to attend with
🎯 Set a specific goal before arriving
😰

Stressful Periods

Exams, family problems, or overwhelming schedules that disrupt healthy routines

Your Action Plan:

🧘‍♀️ Practice 5-minute daily stress relief
📱 Use apps for quick healthy meal ideas
👥 Reach out to support network early
📝 Keep a simple habit tracker
📅

Schedule Changes

Summer break, new school year, or routine disruptions that break established habits

Your Action Plan:

🗓️ Plan new routine 1 week in advance
🎯 Focus on 1-2 core habits initially
⏰ Set phone reminders for new schedule
🔄 Be flexible and adjust as needed
📉

Motivation Drops

Periods when you feel unmotivated, bored, or questioning why you started

Your Action Plan:

📖 Review your "why" and original goals
🏆 Celebrate small wins and progress
🔄 Try new activities to regain interest
💬 Talk to someone who supports you
👥

Peer Pressure

Friends or social groups that don't support or actively discourage healthy choices

Your Action Plan:

🗣️ Practice saying "no" with confidence
🎯 Suggest alternative activities
🤝 Find friends with similar values
💪 Remember your health is YOUR priority
⚡

After a Setback

When you've "slipped up" or had a bad day/week and feel like giving up

Your Action Plan:

💙 Practice self-compassion, not self-criticism
🔍 Analyze what triggered the setback
🚀 Start fresh immediately, don't wait
📞 Reach out for support if needed
🛡️ Prevention tip: Having a plan for 3+ scenarios increases success rate by 67%!

🔥 Maintaining Long-Term Motivation

Build Your Motivation Maintenance System

Click each strategy to explore actions you can take to keep your motivation strong over time

🌈

Keep It Fresh

Regularly introduce variety to prevent boredom and maintain engagement with your healthy habits.

Action Items:

Try a new healthy recipe each week
Explore different types of physical activities
Change workout routes or environments
Set new mini-challenges monthly
👥

Social Connection

Build and maintain relationships with people who support and share your health goals.

Action Items:

Join a sports club or fitness group
Find an accountability buddy
Share goals with family and friends
Celebrate milestones with supportive people
❤️

Intrinsic Motivation

Focus on internal rewards like feeling strong, confident, and energetic rather than just external outcomes.

Action Items:

Keep a daily energy/mood journal
Notice and celebrate how you feel after healthy choices
Focus on strength and capability gains
Connect habits to your core values
🌟

Identity-Based Habits

Shift from "I'm trying to be healthy" to "I am a healthy person" - make it part of who you are.

Action Items:

Say "I am someone who exercises" vs "I should exercise"
Make choices that reinforce your healthy identity
Act like the person you want to become
Vote for your identity with every choice
🚀 Motivation science: Identity-based habits are 4x more likely to be maintained long-term!
